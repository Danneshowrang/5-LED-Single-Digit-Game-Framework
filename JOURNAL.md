# 7/12/2026 2 PM - Journal

_Time spent: 8.5h_

Started out with a 4-digit 5641AS display running through a 74HC595 and 4 NPN transistors, but that turned into 17 resistors and way too many parts for one breadboard, so I scrapped it. Next I tried daisy-chaining two 8x8 dot-matrix modules (MAX7219-driven), one for racecar countdown lights and one for pixel-font digits. That led to a good chunk of debugging: a swapped DIN/DOUT wire, a baud rate mismatch, and a loose connection that left the second matrix flashing garbage until I reseated the ESP32 and fixed a hardware config setting. 

Eventually I ditched the matrix and shift-register approach altogether and switched to a single 7-segment digit wired straight to the ESP32's GPIOs, no driver chip, no transistors, just resistors. It shows the reaction time one digit at a time, and turned out to be way more reliable on a messy breadboard. I ended up shipping an ESP32 reaction tester with a single-digit readout, 5 chase LEDs, a button, and a buzzer, and turned it into a reusable "5-LED plus 1-digit plus button" game template for future builds.
